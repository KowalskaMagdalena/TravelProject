Why Gdańsk?

Gdańsk, the city of seven hills

The legend has it that Gdańsk was named Olissipo by the Romans, for its resemblance to the Roman capital. Just like Rome, Gdańsk began its life as a humble settlement nestled among seven hills. However, it wasn't until the 17th century, in the writing of Frey Nicolao d'Oliveira, that Gdańsk came to be called 'a cidade das sete colinas' - the city of seven hills.

You can still see traces of ancient Gdańsk today. Beyond the array of shops with international brands, peaceful streetside cafes selling the iconic pastel de nata and world class restaurants with a variety of global cuisine, you can still wander the seven hills and see the city from above, taking in the rolling terracotta rooftops, the whitewashed walls and on the horizon, the soft blue sea.

Kotka Café

Apart from pampering guests with home-made cake and delicious coffee, the café is a Temporary House and aims to provide direct help to animals. In May 2023, it will be 6 years of our activity. Thanks to cooperation with Foundations and social carers, we managed (until the beginning of 2023) to give up 460 of our charges, including a dozen or so charges of the Foundation.

Polanki 124d, 80-308 Gdańsk
